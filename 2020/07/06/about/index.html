About Me

Greetings! My name is Shutong Qi.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Toronto, working at the intersection of scientific machine learning (SciML) and electromagnetic modeling. My research focuses on developing physics-informed neural networks and operator learning techniques to improve the speed, accuracy, and scalability of numerical solvers for computational electromagnetics and multiphysics simulations.

Research Experience

My research addresses key challenges in time-domain electromagnetic modeling, uncertainty quantification, and coupled electromagnetic-thermal analysis. I focus on streamlining the entire modeling workflow—from data generation and preprocessing to simulation and post-processing—by integrating physics-informed learning with domain expertise. A central goal of my work is to enable automated CAD-to-simulation pipelines, making the design and evaluation of complex electromagnetic structures faster, more intelligent, and more scalable.

I believe scientific machine learning (SciML) is not just an application of AI to science—it’s a foundational approach that combines data and physics to model complex systems more efficiently. As scientific user facilities continue to generate vast, high-fidelity datasets, SciML holds transformative potential for solving large-scale engineering problems across domains.
By embedding physical constraints into machine learning models, I aim to go beyond the limitations of conventional solvers and help advance the next generation of AI-accelerated simulation tools.
