About Me

Greetings! This is Shutong Qi.
I am a second year PhD student from Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Toronto, advised by Professor Costas D. Sarris. Before I came to Toronto, I graduated as an Excellent Graduate with a bachelor’s degree from Department of Electronics and Information Engineering, Beihang University in July, 2020.

Research Experience

My research focuses on scientific machine learning (SciML) for electromganetics problems. From my perspective, SciML is a core component of artificial intelligence (AI), and the massive data from scientific user facilities are enabling breakthroughs and significant progress in computational physics.

From 2018 to 2020, I worked as an undergraduate researcher at the EMCTI under the supervision of Professor Qiang Ren. We focus on accelerating computational electromagnetics methods via deep learning.
During the summer of 2019, I was a visiting student researcher at the XDiscovery Lab from Dartmouth College, where I worked with Professor Xing-Dong Yang and Te-yen Wu on smart cloth. After that, I had a short visit to the NDE Lab from Michigan State University, working with Professor Yiming Deng on the project Magna.

Previously, I had experience in signal processing in the project ADS-B and my first research project Multi-Antenna OFDM.
